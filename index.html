If P is the proposition "It will rain today", then ¬P is the proposition "It will not rain today."

Propositional logic is a branch of mathematical logic that studies the logical relationships between propositions (statements that can be either true or false). In propositional logic, propositions are represented by symbols, such as P and ¬P in the example above, and logical operators, such as "and", "or", and "not", are used to combine and manipulate these propositions.
